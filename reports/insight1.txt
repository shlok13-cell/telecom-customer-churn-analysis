(2) do older customers churn more?
--------------------------------------------------

From this boxplot, yes, older customers appear to churn more.

Reasoning:

Customers who did not churn (Exited = 0) have a median age around 49 years.
Customers who churned (Exited = 1) have a median age around 35 years.

But notice something important: the churned group has a wider spread, and the upper age range overlaps heavily with non-churned customers.

So based on this plot alone, the answer is actually:

❌ No, older customers do not churn more.
✅ Younger customers seem to churn slightly more, because the churned group's median age is lower.

Business conclusion:

Age may influence churn, but age alone is not a strong indicator. Further analysis (churn rate by age groups) would give a clearer answer.

(3) are expensive plans causing churn?
------------------------------------------------

Based on this boxplot, higher monthly charges may be associated with churn, but we cannot say expensive plans directly cause churn.

Observations:

Non-churned customers (Exited = 0) → median MonthlyCharge ≈ 95
Churned customers (Exited = 1) → median MonthlyCharge ≈ 120

This shows customers who churned tend to have higher monthly charges.

Business insight:
✅ Customers paying more appear more likely to churn.
⚠️ But this is correlation, not causation — expensive plans might churn because:

Customers feel plans are overpriced
Competitors offer better value
High-paying customers have higher expectations

Conclusion:

Yes, expensive plans seem associated with higher churn, but further analysis is needed to confirm if pricing is causing churn.
